Demographics

The total population of Mie as of October 1, 2021, was 1,750,415, a decrease of 14,839 (0.8%) from the previous year. The percentage of children (under 15 years old) in the total population was 11.8%, the working-age population (15-64 years old) was 56.1%, and the elderly population (65 and over) was 29.8%.

Visiting Mie

The grand panorama of Isewan Bay is magnificent! There is a foot bath at the lookout point, which is grateful for the visitors to get a superb view while having their feet in the bath. You will get a magnificent panoramic view of Isewan Bay and Chubu International Airport. On a clear and sunny day, you will get a distant view of the magnificent mountain ranges of Japan Alps and Mt. Fuji.

Historical Shrine

The Ise Grand Shrine, or Ise Jingu, located in the heart of a sacred forest in the Mie Prefecture of Japan, is the most important Shinto shrine in the country. It was first built in 4 BCE, and the present-day structures are based on the buildings erected in the 7th century CE. The complex, as well as the Uju bridge and torii gateway, are rebuilt every 20 years. The last renovation was in 2013. Ise Jingu is the ancestral shrine of the emperors of Japan.

Agriculture

Maruyamasenmaida is chosen as one of the 100 most beautiful terraced rice fields. It is considered as Japan's most beautiful terraced rice field. You could see the different faces through the four seasons. The amazing view from the lookout point will definitely capture the heart of the visitor. There are so many small paddies in multiple layers and the numbers are approximately 1,340.

Motorsports City

Suzuka is the first city in Japan to proclaim itself a "Motorsports City" and is promoting motorsports at the citizen level. The "Suzuka F1 Japan Grand Prix Regional Revitalization Council" has been established as a joint effort of 37 organizations including the Japanese government, Mie Prefecture, surrounding cities and towns, as well as tourism, economic, and traffic organizations, and its aim is to provide spectators with a more enjoyable, safe and secure environment to enjoy the races.

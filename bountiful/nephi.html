Site Plan

Purpose

The TSU web is a church primary website that provides collective links that support Japanese and non-Japanese parents and teachers with multicultural background by providing links for songs and videos available in You tube, verses found in the church website, and original games and presentation created by volunteered brothers and sisters to be used as a resource for primary lessons. We make sure we provide enjoyable resources that will help encourage the children to participate and enjoy the lessons in primary.

Audience

This is website is open to all parents and teachers teaching primary class both in English and Japanese. It is a user-friendly website for children attending the primary lessons in the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints. With the new technology providing easy access to all users, the children will enjoy the songs with lyrics, watch animated stories of the bible and the Book of Mormon and access easy to follow original games and presentation created by volunteered brothers and sisters. You will surely enjoy this website.

The Site Map of a site is just like it sounds…it is a map of the pages in a site and how they are related and linked together. From the map above we can see that we will eventually have the Home page and 2 sub or child pages.

The lines that connect them all together indicate that each page should be accessible from any other page, it is essentially showing us the global navigation for the site.

Wireframes

Wireframes are like blueprints for making webpages. They should show the major sections of content that will be on the page and the relative locations of each element. In the wireframe below you can see there will be 6 sections to our page:

  1. At the top we have a section with the logo (the box with the mountain means an image) and the navigation bar.
  2. Then there is a banner image that stretches all the way across the screen.
  3. Next we have some text and an image
  4. ...followed by another row made up of an image and some text.
  5. Then one more section of text with no image.
  6. Lastly, a footer containing a copyright/name line and 3 social media icons.
